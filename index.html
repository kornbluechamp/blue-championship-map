How to use the course guide

Quick answers for directions and map symbols

How do I get directions?

Choose Restroom, First aid, Concessions, a hole, or another destination. Allow location access when your phone asks.

Why does the site need my location?

Your location is used on your phone to identify your starting point and show a walking route. The public guide does not require an account.

What do the path colors mean?

Blue is a normal spectator path. Orange is a playing-area crossing that may pause while golfers are hitting. Red is a public street crossing where extra caution is needed.

Why does the route sometimes end near a destination?

Some restrooms, concessions, scoreboards, and hospitality areas are in open spectator areas. Walk from the mapped path to the marked destination while following ropes, signs, and staff guidance.

What if the course setup differs from the map?

Always follow tournament signs, ropes, security, accessibility guidance, marshals, and staff instructions. They take priority over this unofficial guide.

What is the Official Map option?

It is the tournament's illustrated course map for reference. Use Live Map for your phone location and walking directions.
