# Onsite Field Guide

## Capture these first

1. **At least three calibration anchors**, widely separated across the course.
   - Strong choices: clubhouse/main gate, Holes 8/9/10 amenity junction, Holes 12/13 amenity junction, and a Hole 6 reference point.
   - Stand at the exact feature, choose **Calibration anchor**, and save a GPS point.
   - In Notes, say exactly where the point belongs on the tournament map.

2. **Every spectator restroom, first-aid, concession and water point.**
   - Record which side of the cart path it is on.
   - Note whether it is public, hospitality-only, volunteer-only, or accessible.

3. **Important cart-path junctions.**
   - Save a point where spectators must choose between branches.
   - Example: `Junction: 9 green / 10 tee / clubhouse`.

4. **Safe walking routes.**
   - Use **Record a cart path** and walk the route.
   - Stop at meaningful junctions instead of recording the entire course as one route.
   - Mark each route Allowed, Conditional or Restricted.

5. **Restrictions and temporary crossings.**
   - Record player-only, cart-only and closed routes as Restricted.
   - Note crossings that may close during play.

## GPS quality

- Stand still for 10–20 seconds.
- Try again when accuracy is worse than roughly 10–15 meters.
- Take a photo of the feature and surrounding signs.
- Do not enter restricted areas to improve a point.

## Send back after scouting

Upload the downloaded `scouting-data.json` file plus:
- Photos of important facilities and junctions.
- Corrections to any marker in the starter map.
- A publicly shareable updated tournament map, when available.
- Notes on which cart paths spectators may use.

Do not put volunteer-only evacuation procedures, credentials, private phone numbers, or sensitive operational details into the public map.
